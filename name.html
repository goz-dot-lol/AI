The Historical Migration

Historically, the family moved approximately 130 kilometers northwest. While the name was born in the mining and imperial center of Goslar, the family established its permanent stronghold in the agrarian region of the Middle Weser, specifically the villages of Lavelsloh and Warmsen.

Why the move?

Migration in Lower Saxony was often driven by the search for farmland or the upheaval of the Thirty Years' War. The move from the Harz to the flat peatlands of Nienburg suggests a shift from urban/mining proximity to agriculture.
